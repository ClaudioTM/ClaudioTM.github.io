## Research Paradigms
According to Guba, E. G. (1990), A research paradigm is “the set of common beliefs and agreements shared between scientists about how problems should be understood and addressed” (Kuhn, T. S. 1962)

There are three things that a researcher should consider, before conducting research:

What is reality? - Ontology
How do you know something? - Epistemology
How do you go about finding it out? - Methodology

There are also two different philosophical stances or paradigms which are related to how we conduct research:

Positivism is the stance that knowledge should be gained through clear and measurable facts. This means positivism can be considered a striclty scientific analysis.
Positivists believe in a single truth and reality.

Constructivism on the other hand is the stance that reality is a socially constructed concept. Constructivists believe that there is no single reality, and that reality is rather a subjective concept.

Your own research paradigm can absolutey sit somewhere in the middle, and it is important not to class yourself as either a positivist or a constructivist, as both paradigms require consideration.
