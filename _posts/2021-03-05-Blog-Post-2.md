## What is ontology? How is it relevant to research?

In philosophy, ontology is a category which looks at the ideas of existence,reality, being and becoming. Ontology questions how entities are grouped in categories, and how these entities are considered to have existence. 

Knowledge of ontology is beneficial to research, as it addresses existence and reality. This can be useful, as it helps researchers decide which realities of their research topic they would like to draw conclusions from, in regards to the nature of what the research item percieves to be reality.

## What is epistemology? How is it relevant to research?

Epistemology is another category of philosophy, which looks at the concept knowledge. It studies the origin, nature and scope of knowledge as well as the rationality of belief.

The goal of epistemology is to answer questions such as; "What do we know?", "What does it mean to say that we know something?", "What makes justified beliefs justified?", and "How do we know that we know?".

Epistemology is relevant to research, because it makes the researcher question the legitimacy of the research item they are analyzing, and makes them ask questions such as; Where has this knowledge come from? How does the author know this is true? Could the author's truth be different to the researcher's truth?
In other words, it makes the researcher question information and where it has come from, before they take it in as truth.
